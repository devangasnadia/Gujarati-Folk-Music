Overview

Gujarati folk music, is a hereditary vocation practised by the Barot, Gadhvi, and Charan communities. Wind instruments like the turi, bungal, and pava, as well as string instruments, are frequently used in Gujarati folk music. The percussion instruments manjira and zanz pot drum, as well as the ravan hattho, ektaro, and jantar.Gujarati folk music is extremely diverse. Bhajan, a sort of poetry used in devotional songs, is divided into categories based on the poetry's or songs' musical accompaniments, such as Prabhati, Katari, Dhol, etc.

Characteristics

Gujarat offers a wide variety of folk music. There is music everywhere and it can be heard all across this state. There are several geographical and cultural diversity within the state, and the music reflects these changes as well. These musical genres are very different from one another, but they all share the distinctive scent of Gujarati sand, which they all proudly wear including me. The state's most well-known folk musical genres include charani, duha, chhand, and others.

One gains an understanding of the intricate cultural and geographic history of that particular location after listening to these folk tunes. In this state, music is employed for more than just amusement. As a Gujarati, I have seen that Gujarati People go to listen to performers who use music to convey both social and religious messages during performances of Dayro and Lokvarta. Elegiac music known as marsiyas has its roots in Marsiya. Lagna-geets or fattannas are simple songs that are played during weddings.

Influence

The most popular form of folk music which is also one of my favourites is Raas Garba. The Festival of Nine Nights, also known as Navratri Utsav, inspires Gujarati cities to light up with colour, dance, and music from September through October. Gujarat has Navratri celebrations in both large cities and tiny villages. During Navratri, Amba Mata, the soil goddess who grants strength and power against the powers of evil, receives special worship through Ras Garba dances and temple pujas. These dances often start at eleven o'clock at night and go until daybreak at various locations throughout the city.
